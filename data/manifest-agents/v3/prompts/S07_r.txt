10 clinical prompts cycled continuously for 60 minutes, 150 max_tokens,

sysctl vm.swapusage) logged per run.

  metric         start (min 0)    end (min 59)    delta
  tok/s (mean)   39.83 (n=8)      39.40 (n=7)     -0.43
  swap           737 MB           7,326 MB        +6,589 MB
  mean tok/s     39.8 (across all 416 runs)
  min tok/s      38.2
  max tok/s      40.4

  Linear regression across 60 minute-means: slope = -0.002 tok/s/min,
  total drift over the hour = -0.12 tok/s. Not significant at this N.

Swap grew 10x over one hour. Throughput showed no significant degradation
(per-minute means vary ±0.4 tok/s around a flat trend).

This is the primary finding: **swap pressure and inference throughput
are decoupled on M-series hardware with 1-bit models.** The behavior is
consistent with Metal GPU memory pinning — the model weights appear to
reside in a memory region macOS does not page out under pressure. The OS
swaps everything else (daemons, shell, bench harness, background
processes) while the model serves at near-constant speed. Direct
measurement of Metal buffer residency (e.g., vmmap) was not performed;
the evidence is the throughput-swap decoupling itself.

key-rotation.txt — signed key succession for subtract.ing

Problem:
  A signing key dies (hardware loss, compromise, planned retirement).
  Every installer who pinned authorized_signers can no longer verify new artifacts.
  Without a signed succession, the chain of trust breaks silently.

Constraint:
  Reflex 2: ssh-keygen -Y already does it. No new format.

Primitive:
  authorized_signers already supports valid-after and valid-before.
  ssh-keygen -Y verify already enforces them.

---

Format (authorized_signers with time bounds):

  hodori@subtract.ing valid-after="20260301" valid-before="20280301" ssh-ed25519 AAAA...current...
  hodori@subtract.ing valid-after="20280201" ssh-ed25519 AAAA...successor...

  Overlap window (20280201–20280301): both keys verify.
  After 20280301: only successor verifies.

---

Transition record (the chain link):

  File: transitions/YYYYMMDD.txt
  Signed by: the outgoing key

  Contents:
    principal: hodori@subtract.ing
    from: SHA256:<outgoing-fingerprint>
    to: SHA256:<successor-fingerprint>
    effective: YYYY-MM-DD
    reason: rotation | compromise | hardware-loss

  Signature: transitions/YYYYMMDD.txt.sig
