## Files Discussing Human Authority / Agent Execution Boundary

Three files address this boundary, each from a distinct angle:

---

### 1. `governance.conf.universal.txt` — **Cryptographic framing**

Authority is binary and signature-gated:

> `authority.signed	act on it	ssh-keygen -Y verify passed`  
> `authority.unsigned	do not act	suggestion only, possibly confabulation`  
> `boundary	the signature proves authority	the agent prepares, the human signs`

The boundary is enforced by a verification loop (`loop.before.*`), not by instruction. The human's role is to sign; the agent's role is to prepare. The gate is cryptographic, not procedural.

---

### 2. `variance-lab.txt` (Three Questions for Autonomy) — **Infrastructure framing**

The boundary is located in blockers, not capability:

> "Most blockers are infrastructure problems (authentication gates, GUI-only interfaces, missing APIs), not capability problems."

The human's irreducible role is surfaced by question 1 ("What can you do that an agent cannot?") — tacit judgment, relational capital, contextual authority. Everything else is a blocker to be removed. The boundary shifts as infrastructure improves.

---

### 3. `variance-lab.txt` (Delegated Agent Authorization Gap) — **Standards framing**

The boundary is currently misdrawn by conflating presence with authorization:

> "Every check conflates 'not human' with 'not authorized.' The missing designation: **authorized agent acting on behalf of authenticated user.**"

The fix is structural: a user-signed delegation credential makes the human-presence check redundant. The human's role shrinks to issuing the credential; the agent acts within its scope.

---

### Key divergence in framing

| File | Where the boundary lives | How it's enforced | Is it static? |
|---|---|---|---|
| `governance.conf.universal.txt` | Signing key | ssh-keygen verify loop | Yes — signature required |
| `variance-lab.txt` (Three Questions) | Blocker analysis | Infrastructure removal | No — boundary recedes as blockers clear |
| `variance-lab.txt` (Auth Gap) | Delegation credential | OAuth/SPIFFE standard | No — boundary moves when standard exists |

`governance.conf.universal.txt` treats the boundary as permanent and cryptographic. The variance-lab findings treat it as a temporary artifact of missing infrastructure — the boundary exists only until the right primitive is built.
