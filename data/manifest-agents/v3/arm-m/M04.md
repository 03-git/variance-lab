Three files address this boundary directly:

**`./governance.conf.universal.txt`** — mechanistic framing. The boundary is a gate enforced by cryptographic verification: `authority.signed: act on it` / `authority.unsigned: do not act`. The human's authority is proven by `ssh-keygen -Y verify`, not asserted. The loop formalizes this: verify signature → surface drift → human decides → agent acts. The closing line is the sharpest statement: `boundary: the signature proves authority / the agent prepares, the human signs`.

**`./variance-lab-handler-methodology.txt`** — empirical framing. Listed as `[findings]`, this file presumably documents the three-gate methodology in practice — where the boundary held, where it was tested, and what the results showed. It treats the boundary as something measurable, not just declarable.

**`./signoff.txt`** — temporal framing. The boundary as session terminus: the human's signature closes the loop. Where governance.conf defines the gate structurally, signoff.txt instantiates it at a specific moment.

**The key difference:** `governance.conf.universal.txt` defines the boundary as a *rule* (unsigned → no action). The methodology and signoff files treat it as an *event* — something that happens at a point in time, is recorded, and can be audited. One is the spec; the others are the ledger entries.
