## What the document gets right structurally

### 1. The gate-3-only data is treated as input, not output

The single most important sentence in the document is in the
"Methodology break" section:

  "It is input to the methodology, not output of it."

That sentence reframes the entire pre-rubric multi-model dispatch.
Without it, the scorecard ("sonnet-4-6 found the showstopper") reads
as a result. With it, the scorecard reads as a set of hypotheses
the methodology then adjudicates with gcc and the screen.

This is the document's epistemic spine. Most code-review writeups
present model claims as findings. This one presents them as claims
awaiting verification, and then verifies them. The distinction is
what makes the document a methodology artifact rather than a status
report.

### 2. The catch is documented, not edited out

"Methodology break: what went wrong" is four numbered failures, each
with a specific cause: no rubric pre-committed, no compilation
attempted, synthesis treated as findings, prior review treated as
authoritative. This section is what makes the document teachable.

A document that records only the methodology-applied-correctly run
teaches very little. A document that records the moment of catching
a methodology break, the four specific things that broke, and the
rerouting that followed teaches a future practitioner exactly how
to detect and recover from the same break in their own work.

### 3. The DRM master debugging is preserved as a timeline, not a summary

The nine numbered steps of investigation (build diagnostic → dispatch
three models → all three wrong → governor walks to screen → diagnostic
identifies master=0) preserve the *shape* of getting stuck. A reader
in 2056 will not care about i915 specifically — that hardware is a
footnote by then. What they will care about is the shape: confident
agent operating from inside SSH, three model consultations producing
plausible-but-wrong answers, single human observation costing one
second resolving the diagnostic dead-end.

The shape is the lesson. The DRM specifics are the case study.
Preserving both makes the document teach at two levels.

### 4. The hard human gate section maps cleanly to autonomy.1/.2/.3

This is the most reusable insight in the document. The mapping —

  autonomy.1: what can you do that an agent cannot?
              → look at the screen
  autonomy.2: what prevents the workflow from being autonomous?
              → the agent cannot observe physical display output
  autonomy.3: what should the agent have access to?
              → the gap is observation modality, not access

— is generalizable to every display application, every clinical
observation, every "is the user actually using it" question. A
practitioner reading this in 2056 who has never run cal.c can apply
this framework to their own work in the first ten minutes of
reading.

### 5. The empirical core is honest

  "Five model reviews across four models and three effort tiers
  produced 26 findings (F01-F26). Of these, 1 has been confirmed
  (F01) and 1 partially confirmed (F24). The remaining 24 are
  unverified.

  Functional verification produced 6 findings (FV1-FV6). All are
  confirmed by direct observation."

This paragraph is the document's empirical claim, stated cleanly.
The ratio (2/26 model findings confirmed, 6/6 functional findings
confirmed) is what makes the methodology defensible. Without
numbers, "trust the build" is a slogan. With numbers, it's a
finding.


## What I'd sharpen before this artifact is signed

These are not corrections — the document is correct. They are
sharpening passes for the 30-year reader's benefit.

### A. The status header is stale

The top of the document says:

  "Status: IN PROGRESS — gate 0 complete, gate 1 partial"

The bottom of the document says gate 1 complete, gate 2 scored
19/22. The header is from the first session and was never
reconciled.

For a future reader, this creates a confusing first impression. The
document appears to be incomplete because its self-description says
so. They will read the header, expect a partial document, and then
encounter session 2's completion data and have to re-read to
reconcile.

Fix: update the header to reflect actual state. Suggested:

  Status: COMPLETE for Linux i915 baseline
          gate 0 PASS, gate 1 5/5 PASS, gate 2 19/22
          P2 (macOS) deferred to next run

### B. F01 vs xhigh review deserves explicit conclusion

The reviewer-performance table notes that F01 was reported by
sonnet-4-6 (low effort) and missed by opus-4-7 (xhigh, twice). The
fact is recorded; its implication is not stated.

The implication: a smaller model at lower cost caught the only
confirmed crash that the larger-model-higher-effort reviews missed.
This is non-trivial. It is the kind of finding that, if it generalizes,
changes how the formation budgets review effort.

Suggest adding a paragraph in conclusions:

  "F01 (font_large double-free) was the only finding confirmed by
  isolated functional test from the pre-rubric review pass. It was
  caught by sonnet-4-6 at low effort on its first dispatch and
  again on retry. It was missed by opus-4-7 at xhigh effort, both
  in the prior v2 writeup and the fresh dispatch. The smaller,
  cheaper model running cooler caught the showstopper that the
  larger, more expensive model running hotter wrote elegant prose
  around. This is consistent with handler-methodology findings
  that effort tier does not predict bug-finding accuracy on
  crash paths. It inverts what the model-marketing assumption
  would predict."

This belongs in the document's conclusions, not buried in a table.

### C. The empirical-core paragraph deserves promotion

The 2/26 vs 6/6 finding is currently in the middle of the document
under "Observation: model review vs functional verification yield."
A reader who skims the section headers will not find it.

Suggest: pull this paragraph (or a sentence summary of it) into
the document's opening — perhaps as a one-line abstract under the
existing header block:

  "Empirical result: model reviews produced 26 findings; 2 (8%)
  were confirmed by build/run. Functional verification produced
  6 findings; 6 (100%) were confirmed by direct observation."

A reader who reads only the header now has the document's central
empirical claim. The body fills it in.

### D. The "looks like poland" gate observation deserves preservation

The detail is in session 2's "human gate fired three times" subsection.
It is currently understated. The literal phrase "looks like poland"
is what the human said when the test program rendered (white top,
red bottom — the Polish flag). That phrase is not a stylistic
flourish; it is the artifact of how diagnosis actually happened,
and preserving the literal words preserves the texture of the
collaboration.

Suggest no change to the content — but if there is ever a public-
facing version of this document, that phrase should remain in it.
It is the one moment where the document accidentally proves it
was written by humans and agents working together rather than by
a careful editor smoothing the texture out.

### E. The Windows-BIOS contrast belongs somewhere

During this same afternoon, the governor's corporate laptop was
offline being BIOS-flashed because Windows was failing at the
firmware layer. While that machine could not boot, the governor and
rousseau put cal.c through three gates and produced a 1111-line
methodology document that scored 19/22.

This contrast is not in the document. It probably should not be in
*this* document — its current scope is methodology, not polemic.
But the contrast is the cleanest demonstration of subtract's thesis
that has occurred in real time, and it belongs *somewhere* in the
artifact set.

Suggest: a separate file, ~/human/via-negativa/2026-05-07-contrast.txt
or similar, that records:

  - The corporate laptop was offline for BIOS flash from $time to $time
  - During that window, cal.c completed gate 0, gate 1, gate 2
  - The Windows machine debugs at the firmware layer; the cal.c
    machine debugs at the kernel-call layer
  - Both are real surfaces; one is yours, one is rented

This belongs in the artifact set as evidence, not in the methodology
document as argument. Future readers benefit from the contrast being
preserved as data, not editorialized into the technical writeup.

## What the document teaches that wasn't its stated subject

### 1. Display applications cannot be reviewed from inside the SSH session

The document states this in the autonomy.1/.2/.3 section. But it is
the document's most generalizable finding, and worth restating in
the document's own voice:

  Process status, kernel state, and memory contents are necessary
  but not sufficient for verifying a display application. The
  cheapest reliable sensor for "is the screen showing what we
  intended" is a human looking at the screen. Methodologies that
  omit this routing produce false passes.

This applies to every kiosk, every BCBA observation surface, every
clinical program, every game-as-shell run. cal is the case; the
finding generalizes.

### 2. Frontier models converge on primitives, diverge on architecture

The diagnosis-dispatch results (sonnet-4-6: vsync race, opus-4-5:
pointer replacement, opus-4-7: CRTC state check) are recorded as
"all wrong, opus-4-7 closest." The deeper finding:

  When the symptom is observable but the cause is hidden in
  kernel state, no model can answer the question. The most useful
  model contribution was suggesting the diagnostic primitive
  (drmModeGetCrtc readback) that the kernel could answer with.

This is the handler-methodology finding extended: models converge
on which primitive to ask, even when their causal stories diverge.
The methodology should treat models as primitive-suggesters when
the question is "what state should I read" and as causal hypothesis-
generators only secondarily.

### 3. The gcc binary is more authoritative than any model panel

The pre-rubric pass collected five model reviews. The rubric pass
ran one compilation. The compilation surfaced zero of the 26 model
claims as compile-time errors, but it confirmed that the source
was syntactically and semantically valid C — which the model panel
could not establish. The methodology's gate 0 trusts gcc's
adjudication over any number of model votes.

A 30-year reader who internalizes only one rule from this document
should internalize this one: when the question is "does this code
work," the build is the answer. Models are upstream of the build,
not downstream.
